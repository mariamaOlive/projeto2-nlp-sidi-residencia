Giuliani says he'd testify in Senate impeachment trial and offers to 'try the case'

by Mike Brest, Defense Reporter  | 
 | January 01, 2020 08:22 AM
Print this article

President Trump's personal attorney Rudy Giuliani said he'd be willing to testify in the Senate's impeachment trial of the president and added that he would like the opportunity to try the case himself.

The former New York City mayor made the statement while fielding questions from journalists outside Trump’s New Year’s Eve party at Mar-a-Lago on Tuesday night.

“I would testify, I would do demonstrations, I’d give lectures, I’d give summations. Or, I do what I do best, I try the case,” he said.

“I’d love to try the case,” Giuliani said. When asked if that’s what he’s actually going to do, Giuliani answered, “I don’t know if anybody would have the courage to give me the case, but if you give me the case, I will prosecute it as a racketeering case, which I kind of invented anyway.”

It's unclear if the Senate will accept new witnesses for the impeachment trial. Democrats have urged Senate Republicans to force witnesses to testify in the trial; however, Republicans have floated calling witnesses from which the Democrats don't want to hear.

Giuliani's overseas actions have been a big part of the case against the president. He conducted an investigation of former Vice President Joe Biden and his son Hunter. He has also been looking into the younger Biden's work with Ukrainian company Burisma, which faced allegations of corruption while the elder Biden was still in office.

The president encouraged Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky in a July phone call to work with Giuliani and to open an investigation into his political foe. The call set off impeachment proceedings in the House, which culminated last month with Trump becoming the third U.S. president to be impeached.

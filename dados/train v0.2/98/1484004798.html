Hello all, and welcome to our live coverage as Newcastle United take on Leicester City on New Year's Day.

Steve Bruce's side will be looking to bounce back following successive defeats against Manchester United and Everton over the festive period.

But the Magpies face another tough test with Leicester the visitors to St James' Park. The Foxes remain second in the table despite recent defeats to Manchester City and runaway leaders Liverpool.

It is the third time the sides will have met this season, with Leicester coming out on top on both previous occasions.

Brendan Rodgers' side knocked the Magpies out of the Carabao Cup in August - winning a penalty shootout in front of the Gallowgate End - before thumping United at the King Power Stadium a month later.

We'll be bringing you all the build-up, team news, live action and post-game analysis from St James' Park throughout the day.

It was a start to 2020 from hell for Newcastle as they lost for the third game in a row - and four players succumbed to injury to make it a Christmas to forget.

United were second best from the off and their own worst enemy as Florian Lejeune’s mistakes contributed to goals from Ayoze Perez, who relished the chance to celebrate in front of the Gallowgate, and James Maddison.

As bad as that was, the injuries compounded the misery of a terrible afternoon. Jetro Willems was the first to go but he was followed by Javier Manquillo and then Jonjo Shelvey didn’t reappear at half-time.

It meant Newcastle had used all of their substitutes but when Fabian Schar’s hamstring went in the first few minutes of the second half, it left United facing 40 minutes with just ten men. Hamza Choudhury made it three to rub salt into the wounds.

In truth, Leicester were happy to just let the game play out on a day to forget at a flat St James’ Park. Here’s how Lee Ryder scored the players.

Ciaran Kelly's verdict

The rest of this game may seem inconsequential but Steve Bruce will be hoping his fatigued Newcastle United side can stand all after four players limped off - and show the resilience that has been crucial to their response since that 5-0 defeat at Leicester in September.

The Foxes, with their tails up, are experts at ripping apart sides who have gone down to 10 men - remembered what they did to Southampton? - and the Magpies have effectively been camped in their own half since the restart.

This is going to be a long final 20 minutes or so as Leicester fans Ole each completed pass.
